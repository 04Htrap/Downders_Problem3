Physical Barriers and Accessibility Issues:

PWD often face difficulties related to physical accessibility, such as the lack of ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. This can limit their mobility and independence.

Discrimination and Stigma:

Negative attitudes and stereotypes about disability can lead to discrimination and social exclusion. PWD may be treated differently or face biased behavior.

Employment Challenges:

Many PWD face barriers to finding and maintaining employment. Employers may be reluctant to hire or accommodate them, and workplace environments may not be accessible.

Limited Access to Education:

Access to quality education can be limited for PWD, which can affect their long-term opportunities and quality of life.

Healthcare Disparities:

PWD may encounter challenges in accessing appropriate healthcare, including barriers to medical facilities and a lack of providers trained in disability-related healthcare.

Economic Inequality:

PWD often experience higher rates of poverty and economic inequality due to limited employment opportunities and the need for costly support services.
